Both game design and development are required to create a game. They are, nevertheless, distinct, and it is critical to recognize the distinction.

Game design is the "creative" half of the equation. It's about creating an engaging game and then figuring out how to make it happen. It entails developing the concept, building prototypes, testing them, and repeating the process until you have a final product.

The act of creating a game is referred to as game development. This frequently entails a group of people working collaboratively to develop the game, generate assets like graphics and music, and put-up features such as menus. Consider transforming the designs from step one into a finished product that users can play with and have fun with.
